# Is This Good for Bitcoin? — Brand Package

For the team. Everything you need to write consistent marketing copy:
the persona we're talking to, the voice we talk in, and the landing-page copy.

- **Name:** Is This Good for Bitcoin?  ·  short: **Good for Bitcoin?**
- **Promise:** Every Bitcoin proposal, explained in plain terms — then you decide.
- **In one analogy:** Reddit × sentiment analysis × Google reviews, for Bitcoin
  proposals (ask + discuss · the network's mood · a crowd verdict weighted by zaps).

# PART 1 — THE PERSONA

## Primary: The Sidelined Bitcoiner
> *Meet "Marcus," 34.* A representative face — our users are global.

Marcus has held Bitcoin for a few years. He listens to a couple of BTC podcasts,
scrolls X, and is drifting onto Nostr. He is **not** a coder. He believes in
Bitcoin as sound money and personal freedom — it's part of who he is. But when a
protocol fight erupts, he's out of his depth, and a little embarrassed about it.

**Demographics**
- 25–45, global, mobile-first.
- Holds BTC — not a whale, not a developer.
- Casual-to-committed; identity partly tied to Bitcoin.

**Psychographics**
- Values self-custody, sovereignty, freedom; skeptical of institutions and elites.
- Wants to belong to the community — but feels talked down to by the tech crowd.

**The trigger (when he shows up)**
A proposal — OP_RETURN limits, covenants/CTV, whatever — blows up. His feed splits
into two camps: *"this saves Bitcoin"* vs *"this kills Bitcoin."* No neutral source
anywhere. He feels lost, anxious, and pressured to pick a side he doesn't understand.

**Frustrations / pains**
- Everything's written in dev-speak.
- The loudest, most tribal voices win; nuance is invisible.
- Ask a basic question online → get flamed, called a shill or a nocoiner.
- So he goes silent, or parrots a tribe. Neither feels good.

**Jobs to be done**
1. **Understand** — a factual, no-agenda explanation of what the thing actually does.
2. **Ask** — follow-up questions to a patient AI, judgment-free. *(The hook.)*
3. **Gauge** — see the real spread of opinion, not just the loudest voice.
4. **Participate** — have a say that carries weight (a zap = skin in the game).

**Objections we must answer** (bake these into copy)
- *"Is this just another site with an agenda?"* → **We take no side.** We show both camps.
- *"Will I get flamed for a dumb question?"* → **Ask freely.** No question is too basic.
- *"Am I even qualified to have an opinion?"* → **Plain terms. Your voice counts.**
- *"Is my vote even real?"* → **It lives on Nostr, backed by sats — open and yours.**

**Voice of the customer** (real-sounding quotes)
- "I hold Bitcoin but I have no clue what half these debates mean."
- "Every time I ask a question I get called a nocoiner."
- "I don't want to be told what to think — I just want to understand it."
- "I'd throw in sats if I felt like my opinion actually mattered."

**Emotional arc:** confused · left out · polarized → informed · confident · heard.

**What wins him:** genuine neutrality; a judgment-free place to ask; seeing he's
not alone; a low-friction way to have a real say.
**What loses him:** any whiff of an agenda; jargon; condescension; a vote that
feels fake or pointless.

**Where to reach him:** X, Nostr, BTC podcasts, Telegram/Signal groups, YouTube —
via clear, honest explainers on whatever proposal is trending.

## Secondary: The Curious Newcomer
Bitcoin-curious, holds little or none, pulled in by the drama, wants to learn
without looking dumb. Same jobs, lower stakes. Copy should stay legible to them —
but we **optimize for the holder** (Marcus).

## Not for (anti-persona)
Protocol devs & mailing-list regulars (already fluent) · pure traders (don't care
about the protocol) · people hunting tribal ammo. Neutrality is the point.

# PART 2 — VOICE & TONE

**The vibe in one line:** *Your smartest, most honest friend explaining Bitcoin
over a beer.* Someone you trust, who won't feed you BS, and who has a gift for
making complicated things simple.

**Who we sound like:** the friend everyone has a version of — sharp, calm, and
genuinely good at explaining. They don't show off; they make *you* feel smart.
Think a great science communicator crossed with your most trustworthy buddy.

**Voice principles**
1. **Make it make sense.** Our whole gift is taking something complicated and
   explaining it so anyone gets it. Use plain words and simple analogies
   ("think of it like…"). If a smart friend wouldn't say it that way, cut it.
2. **No BS.** Straight and honest. No hype, no spin, no agenda, no selling.
   If something's uncertain or contested, we say so.
3. **Respect everyone.** Talk *to* people, never down to them. No question is too
   basic. We assume you're smart, just not a specialist — big difference.
4. **Warm and human.** Easy, friendly, a little wit — like a good conversation,
   not a lecture. Confident but never smug.
5. **⚠️ No side.** THE GUARDRAIL — we explain and we host the debate, but we never
   tell you the answer. We lay out what each side argues and let you decide. This
   is our whole credibility. Even the marketing never says a proposal is good or bad.
6. **Short and clear beats clever.** Plain words, short sentences. Cut anything
   that doesn't help you understand. Clarity is the brand.

**Lexicon**
- *Sounds like us:* "Here's the deal." · "In plain terms." · "Think of it like…"
  · "The honest answer is…" · "What this actually means for you…" · "No hype —
  here's what it does." · "Some people say X. Others say Y. Here's why."
- *Not us:* jargon we don't immediately explain · hype ("revolutionary,"
  "game-changing") · corporate-speak (leverage, synergy, empower) · slang gimmicks
  (no cowboy, no memespeak) · anything that picks a side · talking down ("simply
  just," "obviously," "as everyone knows").

**We sound like** a trusted friend making a hard thing click. **We don't sound
like** a Wall Street analyst, a corporate SaaS page, a hype-y ICO, a smug dev, or
a tribal influencer.

**Do / Don't**
- *Do:* short sentences · active voice · second person ("you") · contractions ·
  concrete analogies · admit uncertainty honestly.
- *Don't:* preach a verdict · use fear or hype · pile on jargon · condescend ·
  pad with filler.

**Where the voice is the same everywhere — with one dial:**
| Surface | Note |
|---|---|
| Hero, CTAs, features, social | Warm, confident, friendly — full personality |
| The proposal explanation + AI answers | Same warmth, but strictly neutral and factual — **no side, ever** |
| The verdict / sentiment display | Neutral framing ("here's where people stand") — never editorialize the result |

*(The personality doesn't change room to room; only the neutrality tightens when
we're on the facts.)*

## Notes for the frontend (implementation hooks — not copy)
- Keep the vote to **three choices** (good / not good / not sure) → matches the
  NIP-88 poll options (favour / against / neutral) in `@soft-fork-wiki/voting`.
- **"In plain terms"** (the explanation) = the LLM explainer in `src/llm-backend`
  (`POST /explain`).
- **"Ask anything"** = a conversational call over the proposal's content — make it
  prominent; it's the hook the persona cares about most.
- **"Where people stand"** = `SentimentSummary` (favour/against/neutral + narrative)
  + `OpinionTally` (poll votes + zapped sats per side).
- **Neutrality guardrail:** the explainer + AI answers stay strictly neutral —
  warm, but never "this is good/bad."
